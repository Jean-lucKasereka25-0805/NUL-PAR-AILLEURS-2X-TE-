The Heritage

The Air Jordan line began in 1984, changing the landscape of basketball footwear forever. It represented a bold move by Nike to sign a rookie named Michael Jordan.

Every release tells a story of a specific moment in sports history, from the "Banned" 1s to the championship-winning 11s.

Air Jordan 1 Retro High

The design philosophy blends high performance with street-ready aesthetics. This is why Jordans remain popular decades later.

Collectors, often called "sneakerheads," hunt for rare colorways and limited collaborations that can sell for thousands of dollars.

Today, the brand has expanded beyond basketball into football, soccer, and high-fashion partnerships with brands like Dior.
